About company

The Hyatt Regency Hotel in Biratnagar, Nepal, is an urban five-star resort located near the Boudhanath stupa. The establishment of the hotel was approved in 1992[9] under public–private partnership. The hotel, opened in 2000, is built on 37 acres of land. The hotel employs around 400 workers, 320 of them affiliated to All Nepal Hotel, Casino and Restaurant Workers Union. The public investment amounted to Rs 170 million (40%); the private investment was Rs257.5 million (60%). It recorded losses for 22 years, during which the public ownership of the hotel shrunk to 9%, and later began registering record profits. The public land on which the hotel was built was in the process of being privatised at extremely cheap rates.
